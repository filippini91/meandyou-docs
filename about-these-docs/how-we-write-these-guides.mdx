## Who these docs are for

The main reader is an **engaged couple** planning their own wedding. Most have never used a wedding-planning app before, so we write in plain language: short sentences, no jargon, no internal product or database terms.

A secondary reader is a **wedding guest** who lands on a page under **For Your Guests**, usually from a link in an email or from the couple directly. Those pages are shorter and assume even less context.

<Tip>
  If you're not sure whether something is "too technical," read it out loud. If it sounds like an engineer wrote it, simplify it.
</Tip>

Each group maps to a real part of the product. When a feature changes in the app, its matching page should change in the same pass — treat this the same way the team treats keeping `messages/en.json` translations in sync with new UI strings.

## Writing conventions

- **Second person, active voice, present tense.** Sentence case for headings ("Getting started", not "Getting Started").
- **No manual H1.** The page title comes from the `title` frontmatter field; the body starts at `##`.
- **One task per page.** If a page is trying to explain two unrelated things, split it.
- **Plain terms, not internal ones.** Say "your wedding", not "event". Say "your invitation link", not "RSVP token". Say "co-planner" for an invited partner/helper (that matches the in-app wording).
- **Every page needs frontmatter**: `title`, `sidebarTitle`, `description`.
- **Use structure, not walls of text**: `<Steps>` for sequences, `<Note>` / `<Tip>` / `<Warning>` for callouts, `<Card>` / `<CardGroup>` to link related pages.
- **Flag paid features.** Any feature that requires a paid plan after the trial ends gets a short `<Note>` near the top of the page, e.g. "This feature is available on paid plans after your trial ends."
- **Link internally with root-relative paths** (`/guest-experience/questionnaire`), never a full URL.

## Adding screenshots (Figma) and videos (Screen Studio)

We write the words first and leave clearly labeled placeholders for visuals, so screenshots and recordings can be produced independently and dropped in later without touching the surrounding text.

**Screenshot placeholder:**

```mdx
<Frame>
  <img src="/images/<section>/<page-slug>-01.png" alt="Describe exactly what the screenshot shows" />
</Frame>
{/* TODO(screenshot): describe the exact screen/state to capture, e.g. "Questionnaire editor with 3 sample questions added" */}
```

**Video placeholder:**

```mdx
{/* TODO(video): 20-40s clip showing: <action sequence>. Suggested filename: <section>/<page-slug>.mp4 */}
<video controls className="w-full aspect-video rounded-xl" src="/videos/<section>/<page-slug>.mp4" />
```

When the real asset is ready, just swap the `src` — the alt text and TODO comment already describe what needs to be captured, so no rewriting is needed.

<Warning>
  Crop screenshots tightly to the feature being discussed. Full-page captures (including sidebar, header, and unrelated UI) go out of date the moment the app's chrome changes.
</Warning>

## Keeping this in sync

This page has a twin: `.cursor/rules/user-docs-standards.mdc` in the main app repository, used by AI agents and engineers working in the codebase who add or change a doc page from within Cursor. If you update one, update the other in the same change.

## Locale scope

English only for now. The site is i18n-ready, and translating into French, German, Spanish, and Italian is planned as a deliberate follow-up phase once the English content is stable — not something to do piecemeal alongside new pages.
